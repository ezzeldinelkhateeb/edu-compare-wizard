Summary : This figure compares the pressure relationships and equations for two pistons in a hydraulic system, considering cases where the pistons are at different levels versus the same horizontal level.

illustration:
# Left and Center Panels: Pistons at Different Levels :
  • Both panels show a U-shaped tube filled with liquid, with two pistons (1 and 2) of different areas (a and A) at different heights.
  • Forces f and F are applied to pistons 1 and 2, respectively.
  • The height difference between the pistons is denoted as h.
  • The pressure equality is given as P₁ = P₂.
  • The pressure equation includes a hydrostatic term: (f/a) = (F/A) + ρgh, where ρ is the liquid density and g is gravitational acceleration.

# Right Panel: Pistons at the Same Horizontal Level :
  • Shows a U-shaped tube with pistons 1 and 2 at the same height.
  • Forces f and F are applied to pistons 1 and 2, respectively.
  • The pressure equality is P₁ = P₂.
  • The pressure equation simplifies to (f/a) = (F/A), with no hydrostatic term.

# Headings and Labels :
  • Top left and center: "المكبسـان في مستويين مختلفين" (The pistons at different levels).
  • Top right: "المكبسـان في نفس المستـوى الأفقي" (The pistons at the same horizontal level).
  • Variables: f, F (forces), a, A (areas), h (height difference), ρ (density), g (gravity), P₁, P₂ (pressures).

# Analysis :
  • When pistons are at different heights, the pressure relationship includes a hydrostatic pressure term (ρgh) due to the height difference.
  • When pistons are at the same level, the hydrostatic term vanishes, and the pressure relationship depends only on the ratio of applied forces to piston areas.
  • The figure visually and mathematically demonstrates the effect of piston height difference on hydraulic pressure transmission. <!-- figure, from page 0 (l=0.108,t=0.651,r=0.888,b=0.892), with ID c59a16c9-b898-4d81-bc5b-906254ba6569 -->
